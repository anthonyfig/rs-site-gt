04 · The trend is bigger than us

The trend is bigger than us.

This shift isn't a Rootstrap idea. It's being pulled by macro forces already in motion.

Token costs keep collapsing

The cost of intelligence is falling fast. Work that was too expensive to run in loops is becoming cheap enough to run continuously.

Model optionality is increasing

Claude, OpenAI, Gemini, and open-weight or self-hosted models (e.g. GLM) are all part of the stack. We're not structurally tied to one provider.

Agentic development is a real pattern

This era's important tools are being built around loops, tool use, evals, permissions, and human review — a new delivery architecture, not just "AI writes code".

When intelligence gets cheaper, loops become viable. When models become interchangeable, Ground Truth becomes more valuable.

05 · Emerging pattern

Companies already build this way.

With today's technology, the most advanced engineering orgs already run delivery on this architecture: context, agents, loops, verification, and human gates. The architecture is converging; no one is copying our model.

Uber

Rideshare at global scale. Engineers act as tech leads directing background agents that open pull requests; humans review. Agentic development is now part of company strategy.

Amazon

Used AI agents to migrate ~30,000 production applications to a new Java version — work its CEO put at over 4,500 developer-years saved.

Coinbase

AI-native engineering is mandated across the company, with code review and human oversight retained.

Shopify

"Reflexive AI usage" is a baseline expectation for every team; teams justify why a task can't be done with AI before asking for more headcount.

The most advanced teams are rebuilding delivery around context, agents, loops, verification, and human gates.

Rootstrap's move

These companies built this for their own engineers. Rootstrap's opportunity is to turn the same pattern into a delivery model for clients.

07 · The core idea

Ground Truth IS the product.

Ground Truth — definition

A precise, living model of how the business actually works: its processes, rules, data, integrations and decisions. It is accurate enough that both people and AI agents can build on it. The software is increasingly derived from it.

  • The client still receives working software.
  • But the durable asset we create is the Ground Truth.
  • Software becomes easier to generate, modify, and regenerate from that model.

The software is a by-product of the Ground Truth.

09 · What a capability is

What a capability is.

A capability is a larger business function made of many specs, flows, acceptance criteria, evals, and agent loops.

Hierarchy
Platform→ Capabilities→ Specs→ Acceptance criteria→ Evals / agent loops→ Implementation
Example — capability: User Management

Sign up · Login · Logout · Forgot & reset password · Invite user · Edit user role · Disable user · Permission checks · Audit trail — each its own spec, with acceptance criteria and evals.

A story is a unit of work. A capability is a unit of value.

Capabilities are a better commercial unit than hours or story points — but not a fake velocity metric. The point isn't to count them; it's to package, price, and deliver validated units of business value.

13 · Reusable loops

The new unit of delivery is the loop.

AI-native delivery isn't asking an agent to write code. It's designing closed loops that execute, verify, create follow-up work, and repeat until a defined condition is met.

— Traditional loop
Human notices→ Creates ticket→ Assigns work→ Verifies→ ↻ repeats
AI-native loop
Spec defines behavior→ QA agent tests→ Bug / gap created→ Fix agent implements→ Independent review→ QA retests→ ↻ until done

Ground Truth makes loops safe. Loops make delivery scalable.

Reusable loop types
QA / Playwright Bug triage Spec consistency Ground Truth consistency Product decision Regression Integration contract Security review
14 · Human gates

Loops surface decisions. They don't invent them.

Agents should not silently resolve business ambiguity — they surface it and route the decision to a human. Specifications stay central; agents amplify them.

— What the agent found
  • SOP: failed payments suspend accounts immediately.
  • Sales notes: customers get a 7-day grace period.
What it does — instead of guessing
Decision needed
"When payment fails — suspend now, or after a grace period?"

Affected: Billing · Account access · Notifications
Confidence: Low → routed to client / Product Architect
Human gates, not full autonomy

Human-in-the-loop remains essential — for now — especially where business judgment, risk, compliance, or product ambiguity are involved.

  • The fix agent never verifies its own work — an independent agent or eval does.
  • Every loop has stop conditions, logs, an owner, and an escalation path.
  • Jira can be the work queue, but Ground Truth stays the source of truth.

Today, humans approve the business reality agents build from. Tomorrow, that boundary may move.

Eval — definition

An automated check of behavior. Where QA asked "does the code run?", an eval asks "does the system do the right thing?" across real cases — and keeps checking as agents change the code.

Ground Truth is the starting point, not the whole system. Reliable AI-native delivery still requires orchestration, evals, guardrails, observability, and strong engineering ownership.

A5 · Ground Truth blueprint

Ground Truth has a standard shape.

Not random documentation. A structured, validated, versioned package that business validates, product reasons from, engineers build from, and agents safely use.

01Project Context

Business & product goals, users, stakeholders, constraints, success metrics, non-goals, open questions.

02Domain Model

Entities, relationships, vocabulary, states & lifecycles, business rules, exceptions, edge cases.

03Capability Specs

Purpose, actors, triggers, happy & alternate paths, acceptance criteria, permissions, error cases, evals.

04Engineering Context

Architecture, repo structure, stack, existing patterns, API & testing conventions, security, deployment.

05Integration Contracts

External systems, APIs, auth, webhooks, source of truth per field, sync direction, failure & retry.

06Eval Suite

Functional, business-rule & regression evals, golden examples, agent-behavior & data-integrity checks.

07Decision Log

Decisions, owner, date, rationale, alternatives considered, tradeoffs, impact.

Every part carries metadata
Owner · Status · Source · Confidence · Last validated · Applies to
Owner: Head of Operations · Status: Approved · Source: COO interview + SOP · Confidence: High · Last validated: June 2026

If it is not structured, owned, versioned, and validated, it is not Ground Truth.

The client should not receive 15 markdown files. It is explorable, searchable, conversational, and correctable — a product surface, not a folder.

A7 · What makes this hard

Where the model lives or dies.

Pricing & trust

Will clients buy a fixed-price capability instead of hours, with no timesheet to point at?

Reuse has limits

A client's Ground Truth can't cross confidentiality walls. What compounds is our method, tooling, and patterns — not their business model — which needs strict versioning and access control.

Creating Ground Truth

Detecting conflicting truths is easy; resolving them is political. It works where a client can authorize one business truth — so client selection matters.

Talent & org

Fewer, more senior people. Developers become architects. Eval work becomes a discipline.

Quality & accountability

When agents generate, who owns the defect? Evals have to be real, not theater.

Commercial transition

Existing contracts, a new sales motion, and real internal attachment to the old rituals.

AI delivery infrastructure

Agents need orchestration, evals, guardrails, memory, tool contracts, and observability. We build this inside real projects with the seniors we already have, then reuse it — the discipline is making it reusable from day one, not throwaway.
