# Goals & Scope

## Why now
Rootstrap's public site still leads with the **old** positioning — "award-winning nearshore
software agency," staff augmentation, product studio — while `/ai-landing` already tells the
**new** story ("AI that ships," AI Discovery Sprint, fixed-bid plans, evals + observability).
Internally the company has gone further still: the *Delivery v2* thesis says **Ground Truth is
the product** and software is derived from it. The site lags the strategy. This project closes that gap.

## Editorial north star — agentic by direction
The **editorial and company direction is the Delivery v2 deck** (`/sources/ai-native-delivery.html`),
and we are leaning **further into the agentic side** than any current material yet shows. The
site's spine: **Ground Truth as the product; delivery as reusable loops of agents, evals, and
guardrails under human gates.** The June 2026 Overview is authoritative for *current facts and
proof* — but it is **not** the editorial ceiling; the new site should read as *more* agentic than
that deck. Lead with **agents, loops, evals, guardrails, observability, and Ground Truth** — and
let the 14-year agency heritage be the proof, not the headline.

## Primary goals

1. **Complete the repositioning** — from *capacity provider* (hours, staff-aug, story points)
   to **capability delivery partner** (validated, AI-native units of business value, powered by
   Ground Truth). The site should make a skeptical product/eng leader believe the method is real.
2. **Be the proof, not a brochure** — the site is built *using* the Blueprint, and ships a
   **Ground Truth Explorer** so visitors can *experience* "Ground Truth as a product surface."
   The medium is the message.
3. **Generate qualified pipeline** — convert ICP-fit visitors into **AI Discovery Sprint**
   conversations (the current commercial entry point on `/ai-landing`).
4. **Be discoverable by search engines *and* LLMs** — rank for the intent terms *and* be
   citable/quotable by AI assistants that increasingly mediate B2B research (see
   `04-engineering-context/seo-and-llm-discovery.md`).
5. **Establish the Blueprint as the operating model** — prove it on Rootstrap's own site so it
   can be sold to clients with a credible reference (the "dogfood" reference case).

## In scope
- Public marketing site (new positioning, capabilities, proof, conversion to Discovery Sprint).
- Ground Truth Explorer (Ask / Browse / Trace / Validate / Generate) over this package.
- The Ground Truth + engineering guidelines that both are derived from (this repo).
- Migration plan for the highest-value case studies / proof points (not all 167).

## Out of scope (this phase)
See [Non-Goals](non-goals-and-open-questions.md). In short: not a full company rebrand, not a
replacement for HubSpot CRM, not the externally-sold client Ground Truth product (this is the
internal pilot).

## Definition of done (v1 of the *site*, after GT validation)
- Positioning approved by leadership; zero placeholder content.
- Explorer answers its canonical questions over real Ground Truth — e.g. "How does lead capture
  work?", "Which system owns leads?", "Who owns the positioning, and is it approved?", "What's still open?"
- Discovery-Sprint conversion path instrumented in HubSpot.
- SEO parity-or-better with the current site; LLM-discoverability checks pass (part 06).
