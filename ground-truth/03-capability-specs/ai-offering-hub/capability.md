# Capability: AI-native offering hub

> Human-facing unit of value. Executable detail in the user stories below.

## Purpose
Present Rootstrap's AI-native offering around the buyer's real problem — **"AI isn't failing at the
model level; it's failing at the product level"** (95% of pilots never reach production) — and route
ICP-fit visitors into an AI Discovery Sprint.

## Actors
Visitor (AI buyer, personas A/B) · Marketing editor.

## Outcomes / success
- A visitor maps their need to an offering and starts an AI Discovery Sprint.

## Scope
**In:** offering overview (4 pillars), AI Discovery Sprint detail, "what clients ask" FAQ.
**Out:** home narrative (→ home), case studies (→ showcase), pricing reconciliation (out of scope, Decision 0003).

## Capability-level acceptance
- [ ] Presents the four pillars (Discovery Sprint · Readiness & Architecture · Product Dev · Integration) and the product-level framing.
- [ ] "When your product needs to → we build → business outcome" mapping; named verticals (Healthcare, SaaS/Enterprise, Financial Services/Legal, Healthtech).
- [ ] FAQ answers the three real client questions, accurately (BR-12 wording).
- [ ] Primary CTA enters the lead-capture flow; server-rendered + FAQ structured data; WCAG AA; no placeholder.
